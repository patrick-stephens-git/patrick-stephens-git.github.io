Product Development Lifecycle

What is your product strategy? The output of your product strategy should be a prioritized list of actions to work on next. These actions could be:

  • - Improve relevance of search results
  • - Add search results personalization
  • - Simplify user task flow to job posting apply

1) Ideation

Based on your list of actions above you can start ideation: how might we improve the relevance of search results? The product development lifecycle starts by answering this question. There are many ways that you can do ideation. For example, setup a team meeting with your design and engineering partners, provide them with the context of the action you're ideating and any evidence you have (the why). Complete a whiteboarding session where each participant lists as many ideas as possible (the what). By the end of the session you should have a list of ideas aimed at achieving your target action. Prioritize these ideas based on which is most likely to help your target customers achieve their goal and your business achieve its goal. This is your roadmap.

I also recommend keeping a spreadsheet where your partners can add new ideas as they come up.

2) Validation

Now that you have a list of ideas, which are you going to work on next? What assumptions are you making about this idea? How can you validate your assumptions about this idea with minimal effort? Does this idea add value or reduce time to existing value? Do your target customers know how to use the idea to achieve their goal? Can the idea be built? Do you and your stakeholders agree that this idea is viable to build?

At this stage you'll be prototyping and meeting with your key stakeholders to validate: value, usability, feasibility, viability. The output of this validation is whether or not we will devote resources to building a live-data prototype of this idea.

3) Development & Testing; GTM Strategy

If you decide to build a live-data prototype of this idea to test with your users then you should plan to have a GTM strategy outlined for if the idea is launched. This user test will result in whether or not there is clear evidence of value to both your users and your business. The output of this step is whether or not this idea is launched.

4) Launch

At this step you launch the idea. Continue monitoring the performance of the idea. If there is an opportunity to reduce time to existing value then consider prioritizing iterations to the idea in your roadmap.
