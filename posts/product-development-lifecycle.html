Product Development Lifecycle

1) Product Strategy

What is your product strategy? The output of your product strategy should be a prioritized list of ideas to work on next. The product development lifecycle starts at this idea-level.

2) Idea Validation

What idea are you working on? What assumptions are you making about this idea? How can you gather clear evidence of this idea adding new value or reducing time existing value? Do our target customers know how to use the idea to achieve their goal? Can the idea be built? Do you and your stakeholders agree that this idea is viable to build? At this stage you’ll be prototyping and meeting with your key stakeholders to validate: value, usability, feasibility, viability. The output of this validation is whether or not we will build a live-data prototype of this idea.

3) Development & Testing of Live-Data Prototype + GTM Strategy

If you decide to build a live-data prototype (a/b test) of this idea then you should have a GTM strategy outlined for if the idea is launched. This test will result in whether or not there is evidence of value to both your users and your business. The output of this step is whether or not this idea is productionalized.

4) Productionalize Idea

By now you’ve launched the idea. Continue monitoring the performance of the idea. If there is an opportunity to reduce time to existing value then consider prioritizing iterations to the idea in your product strategy.
